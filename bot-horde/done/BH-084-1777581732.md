## Goal
Wire the existing `**Verification**` field as the *output* of the acceptance loop. On accept, the tester sets `Verification: human-reviewed` (human tester) or `bot-reviewed` (bot tester). The dev bot is no longer permitted to self-set `human-reviewed` or `bot-reviewed` on a `User-facing: yes` ticket — those values come from a tester transition, not a self-grade.

## Why
fs-010 introduced `Verification` with four values, but the workflow never produced `human-reviewed` or `bot-reviewed`. They were settable but no path actually set them. This ticket closes that gap: the loop produces those values, and the validator (BH-087) rejects them when self-set on user-facing work.

## Done when
- BOTHORDE.md "Acceptance loop" section (added in BH-083) explicitly states: tester writes `Verification: human-reviewed` or `bot-reviewed` as part of the accept step
- BOTHORDE.md "When work is complete" section (existing) is updated: dev bot may self-set `Verification` only to `bot-claimed` or `tests-passed` on `User-facing: no` tickets; on `User-facing: yes`, the dev bot leaves `Verification` blank when handing off to `in-review/`
- Tester role:
  - Human tester → `human-reviewed`
  - Bot tester → `bot-reviewed`
  - Distinction is recorded by the tester at accept time
- Board renders `human-reviewed` more prominently than `bot-reviewed` (already true from BH-072 — confirm no regression)
- BH-087 validator rejects: a `User-facing: yes` ticket in `done/` with `Verification: bot-claimed` or `tests-passed` (must have come through the loop)

## Desired output
After a tester approves a user-facing ticket, the done card shows `HUMAN-REV` or `BOT-REV`. The dev bot can never produce those values directly — they're a signal that an external check happened.

## Success signals
- All `done/` tickets with `User-facing: yes` carry `human-reviewed` or `bot-reviewed`.
- All `done/` tickets with `User-facing: no` carry `bot-claimed` or `tests-passed`.
- Audit query `git log --grep "Trigger: .* accepted"` returns one commit per `human-reviewed` / `bot-reviewed` transition.

## Failure signals
- Dev bots set `Verification: human-reviewed` while skipping the loop. BH-087 must catch this; if it doesn't, the field becomes a lie.
- Testers always pick `human-reviewed` regardless of role. Detectable from commit-author identity vs `Trigger:` lines.

## Tests
- `tests/test_validate.py` — `User-facing: yes` ticket in `done/` with `Verification: bot-claimed` fails; with `human-reviewed` passes
- Manual: walk a fixture ticket through the full loop and confirm the field is populated by the accept step, not the dev step

## Notes
- The four Verification values are unchanged. This ticket is about *who* and *when* sets each, not what they mean.
- Out of scope: a queryable index of who reviewed what across the project. Commit log is sufficient — BH-086 makes it auditable.
