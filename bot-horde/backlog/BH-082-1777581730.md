## Goal
Add a `## How to test` section to the ticket markdown format. Sibling to the existing `## Tests` section (which describes unit/integration scope). The dev bot populates this when handing a `User-facing: yes` ticket off to `in-review/`. Required content: concrete reproducible steps a human or tester-role bot can execute against the running app.

## Why
A tester opening a ticket from `in-review/` needs to know what to actually do — what URL to open, what to click, what to type, what to verify. Without a structured handoff field, that information lives in the dev bot's commit message or, worse, nowhere. A named section makes it a contract.

## Done when
- BOTHORDE.md "Ticket file format" documents the section, positioned after `## Tests` and before `## Notes`
- `build_lib.parse_ticket` recognizes the section and returns `how_to_test` (string, raw section body) on the ticket dict
- Section is **optional** when `User-facing: no`; **required** (non-empty) when `User-facing: yes` and ticket is in `in-review/` or `done/`
- Section may include URLs, shell commands, click-paths, or expected observations — free-form markdown
- BH-087 validator enforces the required-when-User-facing rule
- PM skill prompt updated: when drafting a `User-facing: yes` ticket, draft a placeholder `## How to test` body the dev bot will fill in at handoff time

## Desired output
A tester opens a ticket in `in-review/`, scrolls to `## How to test`, and finds a steps list they can execute without guessing. Approve or reject decision can be made from the ticket alone.

## Success signals
- Tickets in `in-review/` consistently have non-trivial `## How to test` content (not "test it" placeholders).
- Testers don't have to ask the dev bot follow-up questions to know what to verify.

## Failure signals
- Section bloats — every ticket carries a 50-line test plan. Cap is implicit; if it happens, add guidance to the PM skill.
- Section gets boilerplate copy-pasted across tickets. Watch for identical text across multiple tickets.

## Tests
- `tests/test_parser.py` — parse a ticket with `## How to test`, verify body returned; parse without; verify field is empty string
- Validator test (in BH-087): `User-facing: yes` ticket in `in-review/` with empty `## How to test` fails

## Notes
- Free-form by design. The bot writing it knows the shape; the bot reading it knows the shape. Schema-level structure (a steps list, expected results) would be premature.
- Out of scope: rendering the section as a checklist on the board card. The section is for testers reading the ticket, not casual board scanners.
